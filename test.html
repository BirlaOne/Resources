B. Using ABC, how much overhead cost is allocated to Dept. B?

D. Which items are cost objects?

Solution:

A. Using traditional allocation, how much overhead cost is allocated to Dept. B?

Allocated Cost = 900 DLH √ó ‚Çπ37/DLH = ‚Çπ33,300

Step 1: Calculate Activity Rates

  • Setups: \( \frac{16500}{250} \) = ‚Çπ66 per setup
  • Quality Control: \( \frac{24000}{400} \) = ‚Çπ60 per inspection
  • Fabrication: \( \frac{36000}{3600} \) = ‚Çπ10 per run
  • Direct Labour: \( \frac{90000}{4500} \) = ‚Çπ20 per DLH

The cost drivers are the allocation bases: Number of setups, Number of inspections, Production runs, and Direct labour hours.

The cost object is the item for which costs are being measured. In this case, it is Department B.

Q.10) The following information in respect of a component is extracted from the books of M/s Perfect Computers Ltd. Pune for the year ending 31st March, 2025:

  • Maximum Usage in a month: 600 Nos.
  • Minimum usage in a month: 400 Nos.
  • Normal Usage in a month: 450 Nos.
  • Time Lag in Procurement of materials: Maximum 6 Months, minimum 2 months
  • Reorder Quantity 1500 Nos.

You are required to calculate: a) Reorder Level, b) Maximum Level, c) Minimum Level, d) Average Stock Level.

Solution:

a) Reorder Level (ROL)

ROL = Maximum Usage √ó Maximum Lead Time

ROL = 600 units/month √ó 6 months = 3,600 units

b) Maximum Level

Max Level = ROL + Reorder Qty - (Min Usage √ó Min Lead Time)

Max Level = 3,600 + 1,500 - (400 √ó 2) = 5,100 - 800 = 4,300 units

c) Minimum Level

Min Level = ROL - (Normal Usage √ó Average Lead Time)

Average Lead Time = (6 + 2) / 2 = 4 months

Min Level = 3,600 - (450 √ó 4) = 3,600 - 1,800 = 1,800 units

d) Average Stock Level

Average Level = Minimum Level + \( \frac{1}{2} \) (Reorder Quantity)

Average Level = 1,800 + \( \frac{1}{2} \) (1,500) = 1,800 + 750 = 2,550 units

Q.15) From the following information, calculate Labour turnover rate and Labour flux rate:

  • No. of workers as on 01.01.2024 = 7,600
  • No. of workers as on 31.12.2024 = 8,400
  • During the year, 80 workers left while 320 workers were discharged.
  • 1,500 workers were recruited during the year. Of these, 300 workers were recruited because of exits and the rest were recruited in accordance with expansion plans.

Solution:

Data Analysis:

  • Separations: 80 left + 320 discharged = 400
  • Accessions (Recruited): 1,500
  • Replacements (due to exits): 300

Step 1: Calculate Average Number of Workers

Average = (7,600 + 8,400) / 2 = 16,000 / 2 = 8,000 workers

Step 2: Calculate Turnover Rates

Rate = \((\frac{\text{Number}}{\text{Average Workers}}) \times 100\)

  • Separation Rate: \((\frac{400}{8000}) \times 100 = \mathbf{5\%}\)
  • Replacement Rate: \((\frac{300}{8000}) \times 100 = \mathbf{3.75\%}\)
  • Labour Flux Rate: \((\frac{400 + 1500}{8000}) \times 100 = \mathbf{23.75\%}\)
